Summary

I chose to use the crickets data set. There are only three variables here. We have a categorical variable called species with only two different species. Then we have rate and temp. By looking quickly at the data, we could see that rate and temp most likely would increase together. After creating a basic linear model and plotting this data, we see that we would fail to reject that hypothesis. There is significance between rate of chirps per minute and temperature. One species, the O.exclamationis, is much more noisy than the O.niveus. Unfortunately there is data for the O.niveus at temps around 20.5 and under but not the other species. On the other side there is data for the O.exclamationis at temps 28.5 and up but not for the O.niveus. This would need to be researched further as it could potentially mean they live in different climates or they cannot handle one species cannot handle cold and one cannot handle warm.
